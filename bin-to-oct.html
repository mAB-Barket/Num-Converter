How to Convert Binary to Octal

Because 8 is a power of 2 (8 = 2³), converting binary to octal is straightforward: group the bits in sets of three from right to left, then map each group to its octal digit.

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Starting from the right, group the binary digits into sets of 3.
  2. Pad the leftmost group with leading zeros if needed to make a full group of 3.
  3. Convert each 3-bit group to its octal digit (000=0, 001=1, 010=2, 011=3, 100=4, 101=5, 110=6, 111=7).
  4. Concatenate the octal digits for the final result.

Example

For binary 110101: group as 110 101 → 6 and 5 → octal 65.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why group binary bits in threes for octal?

Octal is base 8, and 8 = 2³. Each octal digit maps perfectly to exactly 3 binary bits, making the conversion a simple grouping exercise without any arithmetic.

What is octal used for?

Octal is commonly used in Unix/Linux file permissions (e.g., chmod 755), older computing systems, and some programming contexts as a compact representation of binary data.

What if the number of binary digits is not divisible by 3?

Pad the leftmost group with leading zeros. For example, binary 10110 becomes 010 110, which converts to octal 26.
