How to Convert Hexadecimal to Octal

Hexadecimal and octal don’t share a direct digit-to-digit relationship. The most efficient method uses binary as a bridge: convert each hex digit to 4 binary bits, then re-group into 3-bit octal groups.

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Convert each hex digit to its 4-bit binary equivalent.
  2. Concatenate all the binary groups.
  3. Re-group the binary string into sets of 3 bits, starting from the right.
  4. Pad the leftmost group with zeros if needed.
  5. Convert each 3-bit group to its octal digit (0–7).
  6. Concatenate for the final octal result.

Example

For hex FF: F=1111, F=1111 → 11111111 → regroup: 011 111 111 → octal 377.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why use binary as an intermediate step?

Hex (base 16 = 2⁴) and octal (base 8 = 2³) both have a clean relationship with binary but not with each other. Binary serves as the common language between them, making the conversion systematic and accurate.

Can I convert hex to octal through decimal instead?

Yes, but the binary bridge method is typically faster for manual calculation. Converting through decimal involves multiplication and division, while the binary method is just pattern replacement and regrouping.

What is the hex equivalent of octal 777?

Octal 777 = binary 111 111 111 = regroup as 0001 1111 1111 = hex 1FF. This is decimal 511. In Unix, this represents full permissions for all users.
