How to Convert Octal to Hexadecimal

There is no direct digit-to-digit mapping between octal and hexadecimal because 8 is not a power of 16 (nor vice versa). The standard method uses binary as an intermediate step: convert octal to binary (3 bits per digit), then convert binary to hex (4 bits per digit).

Step-by-Step Method

  1. Convert each octal digit to its 3-bit binary representation.
  2. Concatenate all binary groups.
  3. Re-group the binary digits into sets of 4, starting from the right.
  4. Pad the leftmost group with zeros if needed.
  5. Convert each 4-bit group to its hex digit.
  6. Concatenate for the hex result.

Example

For octal 755: 7=111, 5=101, 5=101 → 111101101 → regroup: 0001 1110 1101 → hex 1ED.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does octal-to-hex conversion go through binary?

Octal and hex do not share a direct power-of-base relationship. Binary is the common link: octal groups into 3 bits, hex into 4 bits. Going through binary makes the conversion systematic and error-free.

Can I convert octal directly to hex without binary?

You could convert octal to decimal first, then decimal to hex, but the binary intermediate method is faster and less error-prone. Most professionals prefer the binary bridge approach.

When would I need to convert octal to hex?

When working with Unix systems (octal permissions) and need to express the same values in hex for debugging, memory inspection, or low-level programming contexts.
